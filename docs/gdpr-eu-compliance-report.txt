5. VOICE DATA RISK
================================================================================

Voice recordings can constitute special category biometric data under
GDPR Art. 9 when processed for the purpose of uniquely identifying a
person [1][2]. Even where voice data is not used for explicit identification,
regulators have taken an increasingly broad interpretation. EDPB (July 2025)
flagged AI-generated voice as potentially violating rights and freedoms [14].

Whether VoiceForge's processing meets the biometric threshold requires
legal assessment. If it does, the following apply:
  - Explicit caller consent required (Art. 9) [1]
  - Mandatory DPIA (Art. 35) [2]
  - Possible DPO appointment (Art. 37) [3]
  - Potential high-risk AI reclassification (AI Act Annex III) [5]

Regardless of biometric classification, voice recordings are personal data
and require a lawful basis under Art. 6 [3], and callers must be informed
they are interacting with AI under the EU AI Act Art. 50 [5].
